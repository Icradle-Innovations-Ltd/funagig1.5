Data Processing Addendum (DPA)

Last updated:

1. Scope

This DPA forms part of the Terms and governs FunaGig’s processing of personal data on behalf of Customers under applicable data protection laws (e.g., GDPR, UK GDPR, and similar frameworks).

2. Roles

The Customer is the Data Controller; FunaGig is the Data Processor. Each party shall comply with its respective obligations under data protection laws.

3. Processing Details

  • Subject Matter: Provision of the FunaGig platform and related services.
  • Duration: For the term of the Customer’s use of the Service.
  • Nature and Purpose: Hosting, transmission, storage, and management of account and platform data to enable the Service.
  • Types of Personal Data: Name, email, role, and optional profile information (e.g., university/major or industry), usage metadata.
  • Data Subjects: Customer personnel, students, and business users.

4. Processor Obligations

  • Process personal data only on documented instructions from the Controller.
  • Maintain appropriate technical and organizational security measures.
  • Ensure personnel confidentiality and limit access on a need‑to‑know basis.
  • Assist the Controller with data subject requests and security obligations where feasible.
  • Notify the Controller without undue delay after becoming aware of a personal data breach.

5. Sub‑processors

FunaGig may engage sub‑processors to support the Service. A current list is maintained in the Privacy Policy. Processor remains responsible for sub‑processors’ obligations.

6. International Transfers

Where personal data is transferred internationally, Processor will implement appropriate safeguards (e.g., SCCs) as required by applicable law.

7. Data Return/Deletion

Upon termination, Processor will delete or return personal data at Controller’s choice, unless retention is required by law.

8. Audit

Processor shall make available information necessary to demonstrate compliance and allow for audits by the Controller or an auditor mandated by the Controller, subject to reasonable limits to protect confidentiality and security.
